Berani – Child Safety & CSAE Standards

Last updated: 24 Aug 2025

Berani is an anti-bullying reporting tool. We have a zero-tolerance policy toward child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) and any content that sexualizes minors.

Scope & Zero-Tolerance

  • Berani does not host public user-generated content or social feeds.
  • Any imagery a user selects is used only to compile a private report on their device unless the user chooses to share it.
  • We strictly prohibit CSAM, sexualized depictions of minors, grooming, sexual extortion, or trading of imagery.

How Berani Handles Images

  • Images are accessed only when a user explicitly selects them through the system photo picker.
  • Images are shown in the report preview and stored in the user’s private Vault if they choose to save.
  • If the user requests an AI summary, only the data necessary to generate the summary is transmitted securely.

Detection, Reporting & Cooperation

  • If we become aware of suspected CSAM through in-app reports, support channels, or lawful requests, we will preserve relevant information and report to appropriate authorities. For U.S. matters, we report to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline.
  • We cooperate with law enforcement subject to applicable laws and due process.
  • If anyone is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services immediately.

User Guidance

  • Use Berani only to document incidents you have a lawful right to report.
  • Do not upload, share, or circulate illegal content. Instead, report suspected CSAM directly to the authorities.
  • Share reports only with trusted adults, safeguarding leads, or relevant officials.

Privacy, Retention & Deletion

  • Reports remain on the device unless you choose to share them.
  • Users can delete images and reports from the Vault at any time.
  • We minimize data collection and retain only what is necessary for support or legal compliance.
